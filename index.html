About Me

Technology has always caught my attention. I've always been the kind of person who wants to know how things work, why something behaves a certain way, and how small changes can make a real difference. That curiosity has followed me my whole life, and it's a big part of who I am.

At Indiana University, I'm studying Informatics with minors in Intelligent Systems Engineering and Business. I like being in a space where I can look at technology from different sides. I enjoy understanding people, systems, and the small details that make something genuinely useful. I've always been good at taking something complicated and breaking it down until it finally makes sense.

I'm drawn to work that has purpose. I like helping people make sense of complex things and finding solutions that actually support them. I care about how technology impacts everyday life, and I try to approach things with a mindset that's practical, thoughtful, and focused on real value.

Outside of classes, I stay involved and keep myself growing. I'm steady, reliable, and I try to be someone people can count on. I like good conversations, good challenges, and environments where people push each other in a positive way.

I'm at a point in my life where I'm focused on becoming the best version of myself. I want to build a life and career that let me keep learning, stay curious, and surround myself with people who aim higher. More than anything, I want to do work that feels meaningful and has a positive impact on the people around me. I'm still figuring out the exact path, but I know I'm moving in a direction that fits me, and I'm excited for what comes next.

Mission & Values

Why Technology Consulting

Engineering is deeply human. Every decision we make as technologists affects communities, environments, and future generations. This understanding drives my passion for technology consulting - it's where technical innovation meets human impact, and where doing the job right means more than just delivering solutions.

In consulting, I see the opportunity to bridge the gap between what's technically possible and what's ethically acceptable. Technology should serve people, not the other way around. My mission is to help organizations implement solutions that are not only efficient and innovative but also responsible and sustainable.

Core Values That Guide My Work

Responsibility & Trust

People rely on technologists to design systems that are safe, fair, and sustainable. This trust is earned through consistent integrity and transparency. Every recommendation I make considers not just immediate business needs, but long-term impacts on all stakeholders.

Balanced Decision-Making

Real-world consulting involves complex trade-offs between cost, safety, environmental impact, and social outcomes. I believe in looking beyond the technical specifications to ask critical questions: Who does this impact? Who benefits? What are the unintended consequences? True value comes from finding solutions that balance business objectives with human and environmental protection.

Courage to Lead

The Challenger disaster taught us that knowing what's right isn't enough - you need the courage to act on it. In consulting, this means being willing to deliver difficult truths, challenge assumptions, and advocate for ethical solutions even when they're not the easiest path. Leadership sometimes means standing alone for what's right.

Technology's Ethical Frontier

As we enter an era dominated by AI and data-driven systems, the ethical stakes have never been higher. Questions of algorithmic bias, privacy, and accountability aren't just technical challenges - they're fundamental to building trust in technology. My approach to consulting addresses these concerns head-on:

  • AI Ethics: Ensuring systems treat people fairly and transparently
  • Sustainability: Designing solutions that reduce environmental impact
  • Accessibility: Building technology that serves everyone, not just the few
  • Long-term Thinking: Considering the lasting effects of today's decisions

My Commitment

Ethics isn't a checklist to review at project completion - it's integrated into every phase of the consulting process. From initial stakeholder meetings to final implementation, I believe in maintaining awareness of how our work connects to the bigger picture.

My goal is to be a consultant who delivers exceptional technical solutions while never losing sight of the human element. By combining strategic thinking with ethical responsibility, I aim to help organizations build technology that makes life better - not just for now, but for the future.
